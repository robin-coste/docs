## Overview

The Audits section helps you prepare for, execute, and track compliance audits. Organize evidence, collaborate with auditors, and maintain audit history.

## Audit Lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Planning">
    Schedule the audit and define scope with your auditor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preparation">
    Collect evidence and prepare documentation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fieldwork">
    Auditor reviews evidence and conducts testing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reporting">
    Receive audit report and address findings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Closure">
    Complete remediation and close the audit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating an Audit

1. Navigate to **Compliance** â†’ **Audits**
2. Click **Create Audit**
3. Fill in audit details:
   - **Name** - Descriptive name (e.g., "SOC 2 Type II 2024")
   - **Framework** - Select the target framework
   - **Period** - Start and end dates of audit period
   - **Auditor** - External audit firm
   - **Due Date** - Target completion date

## Evidence Bundles

Evidence bundles organize documentation for auditor review:

### Creating a Bundle

1. Open the audit
2. Click **Create Evidence Bundle**
3. Select controls to include
4. Add relevant evidence to each control
5. Review and finalize

### Bundle Contents

Each bundle contains:

- **Control Listing** - All in-scope controls
- **Evidence** - Documents, screenshots, exports
- **Test Results** - Automated and manual test outcomes
- **Notes** - Context and explanations

## Auditor Portal

External auditors access evidence through a secure portal:

### Portal Features

- **Read-Only Access** - Auditors cannot modify data
- **Evidence Review** - Browse and download evidence
- **Request System** - Request additional information
- **Comment Thread** - Communication with compliance team

### Inviting Auditors

1. Open the audit
2. Click **Manage Auditors**
3. Enter auditor email addresses
4. Select permission level
5. Send invitation

<Warning>
  Auditor accounts are separate from regular user accounts. Auditors only see audit-related information.
</Warning>

## Managing Requests

### Information Requests

Auditors may request additional information:

1. Review request in the audit dashboard
2. Gather required evidence or documentation
3. Upload response and mark as addressed
4. Auditor reviews and closes request

### Request Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Open** | Awaiting response |
| **Submitted** | Response provided, awaiting review |
| **Accepted** | Auditor satisfied |
| **Needs Revision** | Additional information required |

## Findings and Remediation

### Audit Findings

Auditors may identify findings:

- **Observations** - Minor issues or recommendations
- **Exceptions** - Control failures during audit period
- **Deficiencies** - Significant control weaknesses

### Remediation Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Finding">
    Understand the finding and its impact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Remediation Plan">
    Document steps to address the finding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Implement Changes">
    Execute the remediation plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect Evidence">
    Document the remediation actions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close Finding">
    Submit evidence and close the finding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Audit History

### Past Audits

View completed audits:

- **Audit Reports** - Final audit reports
- **Evidence Archives** - Point-in-time evidence
- **Findings History** - Past findings and remediation

### Continuous Improvement

Use audit history to:

- Track improvement over time
- Identify recurring issues
- Prepare for future audits

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start Early" icon="clock">
    Begin audit preparation at least 2-3 months before fieldwork. This provides time to address gaps and gather comprehensive evidence.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Maintain Ongoing Readiness" icon="sync">
    Don't treat compliance as a point-in-time activity. Continuously collect evidence and maintain controls throughout the year.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Communicate Proactively" icon="comments">
    Keep your auditor informed of any changes or issues. Surprises during fieldwork create delays and complications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document Everything" icon="file-alt">
    When in doubt, document it. Better to have too much evidence than too little.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
